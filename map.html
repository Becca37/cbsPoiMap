This map contains places we've been and places we might someday want to go. It's mainly meant so that we can look up places when we're out and about, but others might find some interesting places to visit too. Many, many more POIs yet to be added! Happy browsing. :0)
Map Controls
  • Show/Hide POI Markers
    • To toggle POIs in a particular category, click that category's Icon control (see legend at end of instructions for details).*
    • To toggle POIs we have visited personally, click the Visited control.*
    • To toggle POIs we have not yet visited personally, click the Not Visited control.*
    • To show ALL POIs, click the Show All control. This is useful if you've hidden a bunch of categories and want to show everything again with one click.
    • To hide ALL POIs, click the Hide All control. This is useful if you only want to see one or more categories, e.g. Dog Parks, and don't want to have to turn every other category off one by one. First click this, then click the one(s) you do want to see.
    * Note that the visited, not visited, and category-specific controls toggle markers in conjunction with one another. e.g. if you hide visited icons one or more categories which are being shown might have no markers. Thus, it's possible to have a map showing no markers depending on the combination selected.
  • Clustering To toggle the clustering of markers, click the clustering control.
  • Traffic To toggle the traffic layer, click the traffic control.
  • Troubleshooting To toggle troubleshooting, click the test control.
  • Location To show your current location click the locator control (bottom left area). You will need to allow the browser to use your location. This will then:
    • Display the GPS coordinates and accuracy of your location in the locator control area.
    • Start watching your movements (less stalker-y than it sounds: location data is not stored).
    • Display your approximate current location on the map with a pulsating blue circle:
    Once location tracking has been started you can:
    • Click the current location icon on the map to show additional information in this side panel about, yup, you guessed it, your current location.
    • Click the crosshairs again at any time to pan/zoom to your current location.
    • Click the close icon to stop location tracking (icon only displays when location tracking is active).
Compatibility / Requirements This does require an internet connection. Use it on a device with GPS if you're concerned about getting the best location accuracy. It should work on current browsers, both desktop and mobile. However, we make no promises about it being pretty or easy to work with on small screens*, on any given device, or on any given operation system. We have used it ourselves on Chrome (desktop, android Galaxy Note 9, iOS iPad Pro), Firefox (desktop), and Safari (iPad iPad Pro).

* If you are on a small device such as a smartphone, landscape mode and pinch-and-zoom is your friend. That or a magnifying glass.
